# Arc 01 — First Contact
### Hugo Rekrot · Story Arc

> **Status:** Refined draft — canonical foundation. Details are locked unless
> a later arc retcons them deliberately. Open threads are listed at the end.

---

## Scene 1 — Outskirts of Voltas, Car Charging Station, Night

The charging station sat at the edge of the highway like an afterthought — four
posts, two working lights, and a vending machine that had been out of order for
six months. Hugo's car hummed at the only occupied port, the progress bar on the
dashboard crawling toward sixty percent.

His phone rang. He answered on the first buzz.

**Hugo:** "Yeah, I'm almost there. Charging up — shouldn't be long."

He ended the call and sat with it for a moment, the city of Voltas glowing on
the horizon. The distance made it look cleaner than it was.

*Albert still doesn't get it.* He turned the thought over. *The blueprint wasn't
a weapon. The application is what makes it a weapon. Anyone with a first-year
physics background could see the distinction — which, apparently, is the problem.
The military doesn't hire people who see distinctions. They hire people who see
targets.*

He replayed the argument in his head. Albert had made his case carefully, the way
Albert always did — citing the contract, the funding, the opportunity, the number
of people who'd worked toward this moment. Hugo had listened to all of it.
Then he'd walked out.

He started the car before the charge finished.

The highway was empty. For about forty seconds.

Then the light came — not from ahead, not from the side, but from directly above,
flooding through the windshield with an intensity that turned everything white.
The hum followed immediately, a frequency he felt in his molars more than he heard
with his ears.

**Hugo:** "What the—"

The wheel locked. The car slid sideways across two lanes. The barrier came up fast,
and then there was nothing.

## Scene 2 — Inside the Ship, Detention Room

He came back to consciousness in that particular way that tells you something went
very wrong — the kind of waking up where the first thirty seconds are just
inventory. Arms. Legs. Head. Intact, all of it. Floor under him, smooth and cool
and metallic in a way that no concrete or composite he knew felt like.

*Not a hospital.*

He sat up slowly, taking in the room. Blue-shifted ambient light from no visible
source. A faint subsonic hum that was either machinery or structural resonance. The
air tasted recycled — filtered past the point of character, the way air in sealed
environments always did.

He stood. Checked his hands out of habit. No blood. No obvious fractures. He
catalogued this as fortunate and moved on.

The door opened before he could reach it.

Three figures stepped through. Tall — roughly human-proportioned but wrong in
several small ways that added up to something significant. Their eyes carried a
faint luminescence. Their skin had a texture that shifted slightly when the light
caught it at different angles. They spoke immediately, rapidly, to each other, not
to him — a language that carried cadence and emphasis in patterns that had nothing
to do with the phonemic structures Hugo knew.

He took a step back. Assessed the room. Assessed them. Calculated the distance to
the door behind them.

Then one lunged.

It was a fast, coordinated strike — clearly rehearsed, clearly intended to
overwhelm. It would have worked on someone who hadn't spent three years developing
a battle suit by learning exactly how much force the human body could absorb and
redirect. Hugo's response was mechanical: sidestep, control the arm, redirect the
momentum past him. The second figure was already moving. He ducked under the swing,
used the attacker's own extension against him, heard the third one coming from the
left by the sound of the footfall and adjusted accordingly.

Thirty seconds. Then three figures on the floor.

Hugo stood in the middle of the room, chest rising and falling, hands still up.
He held the position for a moment, then lowered them.

**Hugo:** "Your approach vectors were fine. The timing was the variable. The
third strike telegraphed about half a second early — that's a recoverable margin
in most scenarios, but not if the target is paying attention. Which I was."

He paused.

**Hugo:** "I don't know if you understood any of that."

A new figure stepped through the door. The contrast was immediate. Where the three
on the floor had moved like a coordinated unit, this one moved like someone who had
long since stopped needing to prove anything. Calm. Unhurried. His eyes moved
across the room in one sweep — the fallen three, then Hugo — and settled with
something that wasn't quite surprise and wasn't quite respect but occupied the
space between them.

**Celan:** "Hugo Rekrot."

Not a question.

**Hugo:** "That's my name, yes. I'm going to need some context."

**Celan:** "You'll have it. I'm Celan — Commander of Fleet 13, Sekkarian Defense
Force." He glanced briefly at the three on the floor. "Ignore them. They had a
theory about you. They're in the process of revising it."

**Hugo:** "The theory being that I couldn't handle myself."

**Celan:** "The theory being that the stories were exaggerated." He said it without
visible amusement, but something in his tone suggested he was enjoying this more
than he was showing. "Want to see something?"

He turned and walked back toward the door without waiting for an answer.

Hugo looked at the three on the floor. Then at the door. Then he followed.

## Scene 3 — Corridor, Sekkarian Ship

The corridor was long and curved slightly upward at the edges, built for a gait
that wasn't quite human. The walls carried etched markings that might have been
functional or decorative — Hugo couldn't tell yet. He filed the question.

**Hugo:** "You said Defense Force. You're military."

**Celan:** "Yes."

**Hugo:** "Then you'll understand when I say the blueprint wasn't a weapon.
The application is what made it one. The design itself is neutral."

**Celan:** "We're aware of the distinction." He kept walking. "We're also aware
that neutral designs rarely stay neutral once a military contract is attached to them."

Hugo had no immediate answer to that. He didn't like that he had no immediate
answer to that.

**Hugo:** "You've been monitoring me."

**Celan:** "For some time. Your intelligence, your work — and yes, the blueprint.
In the wrong hands it represents a significant risk to the balance we maintain in
this system."

**Hugo:** "You maintain balance across the *solar system*."

**Celan:** "We have over twenty fleets operating throughout it. Fleet 13 covers
your region." He stopped at a junction in the corridor and turned. "We intercepted
you tonight for your safety as much as anything else. The firm you left — they
noticed your departure. They've been looking."

Hugo processed this. He didn't let it show.

**Hugo:** "And my father. You mentioned him."

**Celan:** "Sekkarian. You know this. What you may not know is that he came from
a lineage with a long history in this Force. You carry that — genetically, and in
other ways that your time on Earth hasn't fully developed."

A long pause.

**Celan:** "I'll give you a choice, because I think you've earned one. Stay.
Train. Learn what you are. Your capabilities are partially suppressed simply by
circumstance — biology that was never given the right environment." He let that
land. "Or we take you back. You continue your life as it was. But you'll go back
knowing what you walked away from."

Hugo looked down the corridor. The light shifted slightly at the far end, a glimpse
of a larger space beyond. Equipment he didn't recognize. Things he didn't have
words for yet.

He thought about the charging station. The empty highway. The argument with Albert.

The blueprint sitting unfinished in an apartment he'd been renting month-to-month
because he hadn't decided where he was going next.

He thought about his sister. He'd told her he'd be there tonight. They had plans.

*Lisa's going to be furious.*

He almost smiled.

**Hugo:** "I'll stay. But I want access to the lab."

Celan looked at him for a moment. Then, finally, something that qualified as a
smile.

**Celan:** "We'll negotiate. Follow me."

## Scene 4 — Corridor, After the Introduction

They walked further into the ship, Celan two steps ahead, unhurried.

**Hugo:** "Those three who attacked me — what was that actually about?"

**Celan:** "A test. They'd heard accounts of you and doubted them. So they
arranged a demonstration." A brief pause. "Without telling me."

From a side room, the three warriors emerged, visibly battered, moving with the
careful posture of people cataloguing their injuries. One muttered something low
and rapid in Sekkarian.

**Warrior 1:** *"Shis laugh tomam..."*

The second one turned and hit him on the back of the head.

**Warrior 2:** "It's half-human, you *fool.*"

Celan glanced back at them without breaking stride.

**Celan:** "Back to your posts. And log this incident properly — I want it in
the record."

The grumbling faded behind them. Hugo watched them go.

**Hugo:** "What did the first one say?"

**Celan:** "Nothing worth repeating. ZORIN."

The call carried down the corridor. A moment later, footsteps came — heavy,
deliberate, with a faint mechanical undertone that resolved, as the figure rounded
the corner, into a cyborg arm: matte alloy plating along the forearm and shoulder,
with the quiet hydraulic exhale of a joint that had been rebuilt to exceed the
original. The man attached to it was broad, battle-scarred, and looked at Hugo
with the flat assessment of someone who had evaluated a great many people and had
stopped being surprised by most of them.

**Celan:** "This is Zorin — Team 6 lead, and now your direct mentor. He'll
take it from here."

Celan gave Hugo a single nod, then turned and walked back the way they'd come.
No ceremony. Just gone.

Zorin looked at Hugo for a moment.

**Zorin:** "Come on. I'll show you around."

---

## Scene 5 — Ship Tour

Zorin moved efficiently through the ship, pointing things out with the casual
fluency of someone who had memorized a place by living in it rather than studying
it. Hugo kept pace and kept his mouth shut, which took some effort.

**Zorin:** "Residential sector. Living quarters, dining, recreation spaces.
Science and tech lab on your left — you'll get access when you've cleared
clearance. Don't push on that timeline; it'll go faster if you don't."

Hugo peered through the lab door as they passed. Screens. Robotic assembly.
Equipment he recognized in concept but not in form — the underlying physics was
familiar, the implementation wasn't. He filed seventeen questions immediately and
decided to ask none of them.

**Hugo:** "The command center?"

**Zorin:** "Coordinates missions, monitors fleet positions, tracks system-level
threats. You'll see it eventually. Not today."

**Hugo:** "And Fleet 13 operates across—"

**Zorin:** "The region. Yes. Twenty-plus fleets total, all maintaining stability
across the solar system. Team 6 is one unit of one fleet. Small picture — for now."

He said it without condescension. It was the voice of someone who had once been
the small picture himself and knew exactly how it felt.

**Zorin:** "Come meet the team."

---

## Scene 6 — Team 6

They entered a wide chamber. Five figures, each distinct enough that Hugo could
have sketched them from description alone.

**Zorin:** "Luka. Mechanical engineer, youngest on the team."

Luka — wiry, quick-eyed, with oil on his hands and a tool belt that had been
modified to carry twice its original load — gave Hugo a grin and a thumbs-up.
The kind of enthusiasm that hadn't been worn down yet.

**Zorin:** "Dyson. Morter. Muscle."

They were exactly that. Broad, quiet, economical. Morter cracked his knuckles and
gave Hugo an approving nod, which Hugo took as the highest available compliment in
whatever social register Morter operated in.

**Zorin:** "Arina."

She stepped forward, and the room shifted slightly around her — not because of
anything theatrical but because she was the kind of presence that reorganized
space. Sharp features, precise movement, and eyes that read the room before they
read the person in it. And then there was the skin: a faint iridescent shimmer
that, as her gaze settled on Hugo, shifted into a slow violet.

**Zorin:** "Assassin, close-quarters specialist. Her mother is our Fleet Major.
Half Sekkarian, half Pexelas — the Pexelas side gives her the chromatic response.
Emotional state, more or less."

Hugo kept his expression neutral.

**Hugo:** "Nice to meet you."

Arina raised one eyebrow. The violet held steady, unreadable.

He had absolutely no idea what violet meant and made a note to find out through
careful observation rather than by asking someone directly, which would be
embarrassing.

**Zorin:** "And Husty."

Husty was small, green-hued, with large round eyes and a facial structure that
read as ogre-adjacent until you made eye contact, at which point the intelligence
behind the expression recalibrated everything else. He looked at Hugo with the
particular precision of someone who had already done the math on most questions
and was just waiting for the rest of the room to catch up.

**Husty:** "I've already reviewed the publicly available papers under your
name and three of your patent filings. Your secondary coolant approach is
interesting. Suboptimal in two places, but interesting."

**Hugo:** "Which two places?"

**Husty:** "We can discuss it. After you've cleared lab access, presumably."

Hugo almost liked him immediately.

**Zorin:** (placing his cyborg hand on Hugo's shoulder — firm, deliberate, not
unkind) "That's the team. You're part of it now. We look out for each other.
That's not a motto — it's a rule, and it works in both directions. But you'll
have to prove yourself first. Everyone does."

Hugo looked at each of them in turn. Luka's grin. Dyson and Morter's stillness.
Arina's unresolved violet. Husty's patient, slightly impatient expression.

**Hugo:** "Fair enough."

He meant it. He also had seventeen unasked questions about the lab, a secondary
thought about the coolant calculation Husty had mentioned, and — sitting under all
of it, quiet and persistent — the awareness that he hadn't told anyone where he was.

*Lisa was going to be furious.*

## Scene 7 — Voltas, Rekrot Household, Late Night

The house was quiet except for pacing.

Lisa had been at it for forty minutes. She knew because she'd watched the clock,
which was a habit she'd developed specifically to avoid the less productive habit
of catastrophizing without a reference point.

The clock said he was forty minutes late.

Forty minutes wasn't nothing. Forty minutes was Hugo's car breaking down or a
detour or a longer charge time than expected. Forty minutes was a charger port
being occupied and him having to wait for the next one. Forty minutes was
fixable.

She pulled out her phone. Three unanswered messages. One unread voice note she
hadn't sent yet because she was still deciding whether the content was
appropriate.

She'd told him they were starting tonight. He'd said he'd be there. He'd said
*I'll be there*, not *probably* or *unless the charge takes longer*, which was
what he usually said when he was hedging. He'd said it like he meant it.

*He did mean it.* That was what bothered her.

She stopped pacing.

"WHERE ARE YOU, YOU IDIOT."

The shout hit the empty room and came back at her. She put her hands on the back
of the chair, breathed out, and stared at the table where the planning materials
were still laid out, untouched.

Then, quieter:

"Hugo. Where are you?"

No answer. Of course. Just the hum of Voltas outside the window, the lights of a
city that didn't know or care that her brother had said he'd be there by ten, and
it was now eleven-forty, and his phone was going straight to voicemail.

She sat down, pulled the planning materials toward her, and started without him.

She'd be furious when he showed up. She'd also be relieved. She'd decided to
let herself be both.
