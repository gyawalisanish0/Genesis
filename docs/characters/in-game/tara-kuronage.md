## Genesis Orb — Type Yellow

The Genesis Orbs are ancient artifacts of humanity, held across centuries by
families powerful enough to protect them. They are conduits — channels through
which their bearers communicate with the OMEGAS of the multiverse.

Type Yellow carries a dual nature: **Order and Chaos**. Not as opposing forces
in conflict, but as two halves of the same truth.

The Kuronage family has held the Yellow Orb for generations. Tara was not
simply given it — she was **born from it**. The orb split at her birth,
half passing to her and half to her older brother Satoshi. Neither holds a
complete Orb. Together they do.

Tara channels the Orb's energy directly into her casting. At low output it is
invisible — contained, internal. At full power the energy surfaces:

- **Body markings** — a design across her back, ancient in origin
- **Glowing yellow veins** — spreading outward from the markings across her
  skin when the Orb energy runs at full capacity

The markings are not decorative. They are the Orb making itself visible.

## Personality

Calm in a way that is not performed. Tara does not suppress emotion — she
simply operates at a different temperature than most people around her.

She is **thoughtful before she speaks** and **responsible without being asked**.
In a group she is the one who has already considered what happens three steps
from now. Not because she was trained to, but because carrying half of something
as old as the Yellow Orb teaches you that actions have reach.

Her family legacy — the mafia roots, the old wealth, the name that means
something uncomfortable in certain rooms — she carries with **indifference**.
Not shame, not pride. It is simply the ground she was born on. She did not
choose it and she does not apologise for it.

**Satoshi** is the one person who cuts through all of that. Around him she is
warmer, less measured. He is the only one she does not have to explain herself
to — he holds the other half.

### Voice notes

- Speaks in complete sentences, never rushes
- Does not raise her voice — the room quiets instead
- Dry observation rather than humour
- With Satoshi: more open, occasionally catches herself smiling
- At full power: something older and heavier comes through in the voice

## Background

The Kuronage family is old. The kind of old that has survived governments,
wars, and its own worst decisions. The mafia chapter is not ancient history —
it is within living memory — but the family has spent the intervening
generations acquiring the veneer of legitimacy through wealth, art, and
carefully maintained distance from what they used to be.

Tara grew up inside that distance. The money was real. The artifacts were real.
The silence around certain questions was also real. She learned early that some
things in the Kuronage house were not discussed and that asking made rooms
uncomfortable in ways that lasted.

The Yellow Orb was different. Nobody was silent about it — it predated the
family's darker chapters by centuries and would outlast the shame of them. It
was the one inheritance that required no apology.

**The split**: At Tara's birth the Orb divided. Half to her. Half to Satoshi,
her older brother. Whether this was intentional, whether the family expected
it, whether it had happened before — these are questions the Kuronage house
keeps close.

**Open threads (for later chapters):**

  either sibling reach the multiverse alone, or only together
- Satoshi's story and what he would do for her
- The mafia legacy and whether it ever surfaces in the present

## Combat Identity

Tara does not fight to destroy. She fights to control the conditions under
which destruction happens.

Her kit operates on two axes — **Order** (protect, sustain, structure) and
**Chaos** (disrupt, invert, destabilise). In a single engagement she can
shield her team, scramble the enemy timeline, flip the fundamental rules of
the battlefield, and end it with a strike that burns every remaining target.

She is the slowest-building character in a fight and the most dangerous once
the passive economy is running. Her AP costs are high — Phoenix Burst alone
costs 50 AP. Twilight Order is not a passive bonus, it is a prerequisite.
Play the whole battle or the kit does not open up.

**Playstyle arc:**

1. Open with **Intell of Goddess** — shield the team, buy the early window
2. Let **Twilight Order** accumulate as the battlefield spends AP
3. Use **Chaotic Vortex** to displace the enemy timeline and pick a random target
4. Read AP levels — cast **Change of Order** when enemies are AP-depleted to
   maximise damage through the role-swap window
5. Close with **Phoenix Burst** when the passive has rebuilt the AP cost

### Change of Order *(Utility / Disruption)*
> *For a moment, the rules of the battlefield are not what they were.*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| AP Cost | 18 |
| TU Cost | 12 |
| Cooldown | 35 ticks |
| Effect | Full HP/AP role swap on all enemies for 15 ticks |

For 15 ticks:
- Incoming damage hits enemy **AP** instead of HP
- Enemy skills cost **HP** instead of AP

Every action an enemy takes during the window bleeds their HP. Every hit they
receive drains their AP. The aggressive enemy destroys itself by fighting back.
The passive enemy gets whittled through their AP pool.

There is no good answer for the enemy during Change of Order. The only
variable is timing — cast it when enemy AP is already depleted for maximum
pressure through the AP-as-HP window.

## Passive — Twilight Order

> *Between Order and Chaos, energy does not disappear — it finds her.*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Trigger | Every 25 ticks |
| Effect | Gains 10% of all AP spent by all units since the last trigger |

Every unit on the battlefield — ally and enemy alike — feeds the passive. A
high-tempo fight with heavy AP spending accelerates her economy rapidly. A
slow conservative fight starves it.

The passive rewards reading the battlefield tempo. It also means enemies
spending heavily to fight back are directly fuelling her next Phoenix Burst.

## Engine Implementation Notes

Change of Order (HP/AP role swap) is implemented via the `hp-ap-swap` tag on
`tara_001_order_swap`. `makeShieldedBattleState` intercepts HP reductions on
swapped units and redirects damage to AP instead. AP cost deduction on swapped
units is redirected to HP.

Twilight Order passive uses `onBattleTickInterval` (interval 25) — fires via
`fireBattleTickIntervalPassives` after each unit action. The `globalApAccumRef`
accumulates all AP spent by all units; `globalApSpentPercent: 10` reads
`(globalApAccum - baseAtLastTrigger) * 0.10`.

Intell of Goddess shield uses `shieldValue: { stat: 'maxHp', percent: 20, of: 'caster' }` —
resolved at apply time via `resolveValueExpr` so all targets receive a shield
sized to Tara's own max HP.

Phoenix Burn scales with Tara's Power via `of: 'caster'`. The `onTickInterval`
firing context sets `caster` to the original applier (`slot.source` lookup) so
the stat reference resolves correctly even when the burning unit is the target.
