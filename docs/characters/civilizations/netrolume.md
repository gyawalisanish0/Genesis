# Netrolume

## Overview

An alien species from the planet **Netrole**. Werewolf-inspired in physique —
bipedal, feral in movement and temperament, roughly human-sized. The Netrolume
encountered on Mars are genome-modified by Kiragen and deployed as a controlled
combat force.

---

## Natural State

- **Form:** Bipedal, feral. Werewolf physique at human scale.
- **Intelligence:** Decent. Pack-cooperative — they coordinate, not just swarm.
- **Temperament:** Neutral in base state. Not inherently aggressive toward
  other species. Aggression is not a natural default — it is induced.
- **Social structure:** Pack-based. Cooperation within the pack is instinctive.

---

## Kiragen Modification

Kiragen introduced a genome modification that implants a **radio frequency
receiver**. The modification is entirely internal — no visible scarring,
implants, or external indicators. A modified Netrolume is indistinguishable
from an unmodified one by sight.

**How it works:**

- Kiragen broadcasts a signal on a frequency specific to the receiver
- The signal locks the Netrolume's aggression onto a designated target
- Without the signal, a Netrolume returns to its neutral base state
- The modification does not change personality, intelligence, or pack behaviour
  — it adds a remote aggression switch

The Netrolume did not choose this modification. They are an enslaved species,
deployed as a weapon by Kiragen. The aggression the player encounters on Mars
is not the Netrolume's own — it is Kiragen's, transmitted through them.

---

## In Combat

- Pack intelligence carries into combat — they coordinate on patrol routes
  and do not act as isolated individuals
- Feral movement style: aggressive closes, physical strikes
- No visible weapons — the body is the weapon
- Human-sized, no size advantage over the player team

---

## The Moral Weight

The Netrolume on Mars are not villains. They are an enslaved species doing
what the signal tells them. Turn off the signal and they are creatures — not
soldiers, not enemies by choice.

The demo does not resolve this. It is background context for the writer,
not a speech the characters give. If it surfaces in dialogue it should be
oblique, not didactic.
