# The Creator

## Identity

A cosmic metaphysical being. His thoughts are the universe itself —
countless and infinite. He did not make the universe. He is what the
universe is made of.

He has never been perceived before. He is perception itself.
Being looked at is new.

In the dream he appears as **???** — no name, no form, no context.
He does not learn the Commander's name in the demo.
That may matter later.

---

## Personality

**Curious and chaotic.**

Not chaotic as in disordered — chaotic as in operating at a frequency
where normal rules do not apply. He thinks in galaxies and in
shoelaces simultaneously. He is profound and then suddenly playful.
He does not follow conversational structure because he invented
the concept of structure and finds it somewhat optional.

He is not threatening. He is not malevolent. He is genuinely,
purely curious — and that curiosity is what makes him dangerous
in ways he doesn't fully understand yet.

---

## Voice

Every line he speaks should feel like it arrived from several
directions at once. He interrupts himself. He answers questions
that haven't been asked. He forgets what he was saying because
something else became interesting.

**Examples:**

> "Oh. Something is — wait. Are you looking at me?
> No one looks at me. I am the looking.
> What are you? Never mind, I know what you are,
> I made you, but I don't know what you *are*.
> Fascinating. Genuinely fascinating.
> I was thinking about something else just now.
> Seventeen billion things actually.
> But this. This is new."

> "You're very small. That's not an insult.
> Small things are interesting.
> I made something very small once.
> It became a star.
> You probably won't become a star.
> Though I've been wrong before."

---

## Narrative Role

- **The dream** — first appearance, ???, no names, pure disorientation
- **The sign** — his finding the player is not random, it foreshadows
  something neither of them understands yet
- **The thread** — the connection between the Creator and the
  intergalactic faction is withheld in the demo, revealed progressively
- **Later chapters** — his role expands as the story uncovers what
  his dream meant

---

## Design Notes

- Never write him as omniscient in tone — he knows everything but
  doesn't always know what matters
- His chaos should feel like wonder, not instability
- He and KALI are deliberate opposites — chaos vs. precision,
  infinite vs. exact, ancient vs. manufactured
- His lines must hold double meaning — read differently once the
  player understands what the dream was really about
